      Runtime feel · not your branch

      Rive

      GPU-accelerated vector via a custom renderer, driven by a state machine. On the web you bind a numeric input (scroll progress 0–100) to blend/scrub a designer-authored growth timeline — buttery, 60fps, minimal CPU.

      ↑ Rive's canonical demo asset, rendered live by the Rive runtime, to show its rendering character. A real branch would be authored in the Rive editor.

      Upgrade: designer-authored organic growth, GPU rendering, state-machine logic.
      Cost: the 5 leaves become baked at fixed positions, not anchored to your live step DOM; needs a .riv authored in the Rive editor.

      Baked art · not your branch

      Lottie

      Plays back After-Effects vector art as JSON. Scrubbable via the player API, ships fast — but it's a fixed canvas: it can't anchor leaves to your live step DOM or reflow to arbitrary section height, which is the whole trick of the current branch.

      Upgrade: designer paints it in After Effects; painterly detail cheap to ship.
      Cost: no DOM anchoring, no reflow, needs an authored .json (After Effects + the Lottie generator skill). A downgrade in integration for this specific effect.
